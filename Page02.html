Newton, Galileo and Einstein have all been called "Fathers of Modern Physics." Newton was called this because of his famous law of motion and gravitation. Galileo for his role in the scientific revolution and his contributions on observational astronomy. and Einstein for his ground breaking theory of relativity.

Along with Lavoisier, Boyle, and Dalton, Berzelius is known as the father of modern chemistry. In 1828 he compiled a table of relative atomic weights, which included all of the elements known at the time.
